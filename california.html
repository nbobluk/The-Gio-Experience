California Landmarks

California Bio

The idea of this project started with having visited Monterey County's Custom House back in 2018 during a California Coastal Trip stop. After learning that Monterey was the capital of Alta California (Mexico's northernmost province), Gio wanted to visit all of the California landmarks but realized that there were no state or federal websites that listed them in order.

Gio's goal is to help the public find the direct coordinates of each plaque or historical event, so that everybody can enjoy, learn, and respect the trials and tribulations of this specific pocket of Turtle Island.

Landmark Collection

#1 The Custom House

The Old Custom House (Spanish: Antigua Aduana) is the oldest surviving government building in California, built in 1827 by Mexican authorities in Monterey, then the capital of Alta California. The former custom house is the first designated California Historical Landmark, marking the site where U.S. Commodore John Drake Sloat raised the American flag and declared California part of the United States in 1846 during the American Conquest of California.

#2 Portola Journey's End

Near "El Palo Alto", the tall tree, the Portola Expedition of 63 men and 200 horses and mules camped. They had traveled from San Diego in search of Monterey but discovered instead the Bay of San Francisco. Finding the bay too large to go around and deciding that Monterey had been by-passed, they ended the search and returned to San Diego.

#3 Mission San Francisco Solano

Mission San Francisco Solano, also known as the Sonoma Mission, is California Historical Landmark #3. Located in Sonoma, California, it is the northernmost and final Spanish mission built in the state, and the only one established under independent Mexico.

#4 Vallejo Estate

The Vallejo Estate (also known as Lachryma Montis) is located in Sonoma State Historic Park in Sonoma, California. Built in 1850, this was the final home of General Mariano Guadalupe Vallejo, a prominent Mexican-era commander and founder of the Pueblo of Sonoma.

#5 Fort Ross

Established in 1812 by the Russian-American Company, Fort Ross was the southernmost Russian settlement in North America. After the Russians withdrew to Alaska in 1841, the land was purchased by Captain John Sutter. The site was acquired by the State of California in 1906 and is now preserved within Fort Ross State Historic Park.
